Curiosities

Why is the Hulk green?

The color green is as closely associated with the Hulk as his legendary mood swings. That color, however, is just the result of an error. Originally, the Hulk, created by Stan Lee and Jack Kirby and whose first issue was published in 1967, was gray. However, printing technology made it impossible to print the color uniformly, and each page displayed a different shade of gray.

Lee believed it was unacceptable to have a superhero that changed color with each page, and asked the printers what shade they thought might work. In response, he heard that only gray was having problems, so he considered using another color.

Green wasn't used by any superheroes at the time, so he decided to paint the Hulk that way. A choice that ended up becoming one of the character's trademarks. Although, over the years, the green giant has returned to its original color on a few occasions.

The Spider-Man Fan Who Inspired Venom

Did you know that Venom, one of Marvel's most famous characters, started out as an extra suit for Spider-Man? Marvel held a contest for artists and writers and a kid named Randy Schueller entered with an original design for the Spider-Man costume: black with a red spider emblazoned on the chest. Marvel editors paid him $220 for the creation, which became one of Spider-Man's suits and, later, Venom.
